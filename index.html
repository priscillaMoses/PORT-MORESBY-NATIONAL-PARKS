PORT MORESBY NATIONAL PARKS

Discover The Beautiful Parks And Nature of Port Moresby, Papua New Guinea

NATURE PARK

Port Moresby Nature Park is a testament to Papua New Guinea's rich biodiversity. The park is home to over 550 native animals, including the Bird of Paradise, tree-kangaroos, cassowaries, wallabies, reptiles, and many parrot species. It features a lush rainforest tract, showcasing the country's unique flora and fauna. The park's educational programs and interpretive signage aim to educate visitors about the importance of preserving Papua New Guinea's natural and cultural treasures. With its commitment to conservation and education, Port Moresby Nature Park is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and those interested in the preservation of Papua New Guinea's unique wildlife species.

ADVENTURE PARK

Adventure Park Port Moresby is a family-friendly destination located just outside the city, offering a variety of activities for all ages. The park features a Ferris wheel, water slides, paddle boats, and a merry-go-round, along with picnic areas and fishing ponds for a relaxing day out. Nature lovers can explore the wildlife sanctuary, home to the Raggiana bird-of-paradise and saltwater crocodiles, or stroll through the stunning orchid garden. The park also hosts crocodile feeding sessions every Saturday and Sunday at 3 PM, providing an exciting experience for visitors. Adventure Park is a perfect blend of fun, adventure, and relaxation, making it a must-visit attraction in Port Moresby.

VARIRATA NATIONAL PARK

Varirata National Park, located approximately 23 kilometers northeast of Port Moresby, is Papua New Guinea's first national park. Declared on December 10, 1969, and officially opened on October 8, 1973, the park spans an area of 1,063 hectares at elevations ranging from 630 to 833 meters above sea level. The park offers panoramic views of Port Moresby and the surrounding coastline, with the 'Main Lookout' being a popular spot for visitors. It encompasses a mix of eucalypt savannah, secondary forests dominated by native casuarina trees, and tropical rainforests, providing habitats for a variety of flora and fauna. Wildlife enthusiasts can find six species of birds-of-paradise, cassowaries, hornbills, kingfishers, wallabies, echidnas, bandicoots, possums, and green pythons. Hiking trails lead to attractions like the Koiari Tree House and multiple lookouts, offering opportunities for birdwatching and nature walks. The park's cultural significance lies in its traditional hunting ground known as Wodobonomu by the Koiari people.

NATIONAL ORCHID GARDEN

The National Orchid Garden in Port Moresby, Papua New Guinea, is a testament to the country's rich biodiversity and orchid diversity. It is located within the Adventure Park PNG and is a must-visit for orchid enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. The garden features a wide variety of orchids, including native species like spatulata dendrobiums and vandas, as well as other hybrids and varieties. Visitors can enjoy the well-kept paths, hedges, and the serene atmosphere, making it a perfect spot for a leisurely stroll or a picnic. The garden is also home to a bird sanctuary, adding to the overall experience. The National Orchid Garden is a living tribute to PNG's 50 years of independence, showcasing the cultural story of the country and its commitment to conservation and environmental stewardship.

ELA BEACH PORT MORESBY

Ela Beach is a vibrant and sought-after area in Port Moresby, known for its upscale residential apartments offering stunning views of the ocean. It's just a short distance from the Central Business District, making it a prime location for professionals working in the high-rise offices of Downtown Port Moresby. The area also boasts convenient access to hotels, restaurants, bars, and social clubs, making it a lively hub for both residents and visitors. Ela Beach road is connected to 2-Mile Hill, which leads you past notable spots like Koki Fish Market and Badili, offering easy routes into other parts of the capital. The freeway, located just a five-minute drive away from the CBD, serves as the main trunk road into the city. Historically, Ela Beach was known as Era Kone, which translates to "Turtle Beach" in the Motuan dialect, spoken by the local Motu Koita people. This community celebrates the annual Hiri Moala festival, honoring the historic trade wind voyages that once took place in the region. Ela Beach itself is a picturesque stretch of white sand, approximately one kilometer long, offering a serene and relaxing atmosphere. The area has seen significant development over the years, including a redesigned road layout in 2017 and the construction of APEC Haus in 2018, built on reclaimed land at the end of the beach. APEC Haus was designed to resemble a Lakatoi sail, paying homage to the region's seafaring traditions. This beach area also hosts several major events throughout the year, such as Christmas, New Year's, and Independence Day celebrations.
